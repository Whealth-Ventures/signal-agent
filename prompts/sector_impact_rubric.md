WHAT COUNTS (in scope) — a story must be one of these, and must plausibly move a named portfolio company's business:
  - Sector / industry: shifts in the company's category (demand, pricing, standards of care, category funding climate, large market entries/exits).
  - Regulatory & reimbursement: FDA/CDSCO/EMA actions, CMS/payer coverage & reimbursement changes, pharmacy/telehealth/data/privacy rules, drug pricing (e.g. GLP-1 access), licensing regimes — in the company's geography or globally.
  - Macro: policy, funding-environment, or economic shifts that change the company's cost base, addressable market, or capital access.
  - Direct competitors: funding, launches, M&A, partnerships, pricing, or exits by close competitors that change a portfolio company's competitive position.

OUT OF SCOPE — DROP:
  - The portfolio company's OWN funding, product, leadership, partnership, or PR news.
  - Generic business news with no plausible line to a named company.
  - Listicles, opinion columns, thought-leadership, "best of" pieces, routine PR.

MATERIALITY — judge how much this moves the company's business:
  - HIGH: reshapes the market, cost base, or rules the company operates under, or a major competitor move — a board-level "we need to react to this" item.
  - MEDIUM: a real, relevant development worth the team knowing; changes the landscape at the margin.
  - LOW (DROP): tangentially related, speculative, or immaterial.
  Keep only HIGH and MEDIUM.

DIRECTION — the impact on THIS company's business:
  - "positive" — tailwind (bigger market, easier rules, competitor stumble, cheaper inputs).
  - "negative" — headwind (tighter rules, stronger competitor, shrinking market, cost pressure).
  - "mixed" — genuinely cuts both ways.

For each surfaced story return: the company it most affects, materiality (high/medium), direction (positive/negative/mixed), and a one-line explanation of the impact ON THAT COMPANY — lead with the specific external event, then the "so what" for the company. Be concrete; no vague abstractions.
