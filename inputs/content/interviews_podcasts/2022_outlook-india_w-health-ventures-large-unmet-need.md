# W Health Ventures Invests In Healthcare Startups That Address A Large Unmet Need

*Outlook India — Business Spotlight, 2022*

> Body not retrievable in full via web fetch. Summary below is reconstructed from search snippets. See URL for the original.

## Key points

- W Health Ventures has committed to invest a **$100 million fund** across India and US healthcare.
- Primary focus: healthcare startups addressing **large, unmet needs**.
- Pankaj Jethwani (then EVP) framing: the healthcare industry lacks innovation and growth has been slow primarily because of:
  - The skewed **doctor-to-patient count**.
  - The country's **complex healthcare processes** (multi-stakeholder, fragmented, regulatory-heavy).
- W Health Ventures was launched in 2019.

## Why this matters for the W Health Ventures corpus

This is one of the earlier (pre-2070-Health) public articulations of the W Health Ventures investment thesis. The $100M figure spans Fund I and what would later become Fund II. The framing of "large unmet need" anchors the firm's later venture-studio thesis (and the literal headline of the 2023 Economic Times interview).
