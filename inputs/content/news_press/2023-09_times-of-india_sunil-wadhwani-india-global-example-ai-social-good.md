# India can set a global example in using artificial intelligence for social good: Sunil Wadhwani

*Times of India (Sunday Times) — September 2023*

> Content not retrievable via web fetch — articleshow.cms URLs are heavily JavaScript-driven. Summary below is reconstructed from concurrent TIME 100 AI coverage and Sunil Wadhwani's public statements. See URL for the original.

## Key quotes / framing

- Sunil Wadhwani: "We thought that in the U.S., China, and Europe, AI is being leveraged to help people who are already well-off, but maybe we can make India the global leader in applying AI for social good."

## Key points

- The article frames Sunil Wadhwani's view that India can leapfrog into a global leadership position by deploying AI specifically for social-impact use cases (healthcare, education, agriculture, public services).
- In 2018, Sunil and his brother Romesh began thinking about how to harness AI to help solve global development challenges, especially in countries where people were living on less than $5 a day.
- They funneled an initial $30M to the creation of **Wadhwani AI**, with $60M+ committed to date.
- Wadhwani AI is a Mumbai-based nonprofit institute focused on building scalable AI solutions for healthcare, education, and agriculture for underserved communities, partnering with governments in the Global South.
- Context: the article was published around the same time as Sunil and Romesh's recognition on the inaugural TIME 100 AI Leaders list (September 2023).
- W Health Ventures and 2070 Health are companion entities in Sunil Wadhwani's broader portfolio of for-profit healthcare bets.
