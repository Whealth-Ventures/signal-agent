# W Health Ventures announces $70 M Fund II focused on company creation

*BioSpectrum India — July 2025*

> Content not retrievable in full via web fetch. Summary below is reconstructed from search snippets and concurrent coverage across Inc42, Outlook Business, Entrepreneur India, Business Standard, and others. See URL for the original.

## Key points

- W Health Ventures (Boston- and India-based VC firm) announced a **$70 million Fund II** focused on company creation.
- The fund will back **8–10 healthcare startups** over the next four years.
- Two priority sectors:
  1. **Single-specialty care delivery** models for Indian clinical needs.
  2. **AI-powered B2B healthcare services** aimed at US markets ("India for the World" thesis).
- Fund II's first deployment: **Everhope Oncology** (March 2025) with $10M day-zero investment alongside Narayana Health.
- Average cheque size: ₹30–50 Cr per company.
- Pankaj Jethwani (Managing Partner) reaffirms a "company creation" thesis — the firm doesn't just write checks but co-builds companies via the 2070 Health venture studio.
- Fund I deployed about $43M across 12 companies including Wysa, BeatO, Mylo, and Nivaan Care; portfolio now serves >25 million patients globally.
