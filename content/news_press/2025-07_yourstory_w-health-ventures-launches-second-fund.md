# W Health Ventures launches $70 M second fund

*YourStory — July 2025*

> Content not retrievable in full via web fetch. Summary below is reconstructed from search snippets and related coverage. See URL for the original.

## Key points

- W Health Ventures launched a $70 million second fund to build and scale healthcare startups in India, doubling down on its strategy of incubating companies from the ground up.
- The Boston- and India-based VC firm said Fund II will back **eight to ten companies over the next four years**.
- Two focus areas:
  1. **Single-specialty care delivery models** tailored for Indian clinical needs.
  2. **AI-powered B2B healthcare services** aimed at markets in the United States.
- Fund II's first disclosed investment is **EverHope Oncology** — modern outpatient cancer-care centers and global-standard protocols, launched with hospital chain Narayana Health with a $10M "day-zero" investment.
- Fund I deployed about $43 million across 12 companies, including Wysa (mental health), BeatO (chronic disease), Mylo (parenting), and Nivaan (pain).
- Quote from Dr. Pankaj Jethwani, Managing Partner: "Healthcare innovation in India remains 10–15 years behind global benchmarks. This gap is a generational opportunity."
