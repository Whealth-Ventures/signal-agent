# Wysa raises $5.5 mn Series A led by W Health Ventures

*BusinessToday — May 21, 2021*

> Story reconstructed from concurrent BusinessToday, YourStory, Wysa blog, and Crunchbase News coverage. The web fetch did not return the full body in this pass.

## Key points

- **Wysa**, an AI-guided mental-health platform, announced a **$5.5M Series A** financing round **led by W Health Ventures** on **May 20-21, 2021**, during Mental Health Awareness Month.
- W Health Ventures is described as "a Boston-based venture capital firm founded by serial technology entrepreneur Sunil Wadhwani that invests in early-stage companies addressing healthcare needs using technology and AI."
- **Co-investors**: Google Assistant Investment Program; existing investors **pi Ventures** and **Kae Capital**.
- **Use of funds**:
  - Support Wysa's offering to **employers expanding their mental-health benefits** (the B2B/B2B2C wedge).
  - Scale up Wysa's **sales team and therapist network**.
- **Traction at announcement**: Wysa's HIPAA-compliant platform had facilitated **>100 million conversations**, with **>3 million users**, covering **>10 million lives in 65+ countries**.

## Why this matters for the W Health Ventures corpus

This is one of the first publicly documented **Fund I investments** by W Health Ventures, and a marquee one — Wysa subsequently raised a $20M follow-on round and is one of the firm's flagship "India for the World" portfolio companies. The 2021 Wysa announcement is also the earliest external press coverage establishing Sunil Wadhwani as the founder of W Health Ventures.
