# How Investors Evaluate Healthtech Startups

## Six evaluation criteria

### 1. Value proposition tested with stakeholders

Healthcare delivery is a multi-stakeholder system (patients, doctors, hospitals, payers, regulators). New solutions must offer **exponentially better value** for at least one stakeholder, and that value has to be tested by speaking with relevant stakeholders about whether they will pay for or partner with the solution. "Nice-to-have" doesn't fund.

### 2. Clinical outcomes

Healthcare-focused funds focus diligence on **care protocols and clinical outcomes**. Engagement and patient-satisfaction scores are critical to building a long-term defensible business. Financial metrics are downstream of clinical metrics.

### 3. Due diligence transparency

The **quality of the data room** is a critical indicator of a company's internal processes. Healthtech startups must accurately represent their company and stay transparent — anything else hurts trust and slows diligence.

### 4. Technology scalability

A key part of evaluation is **technology scalability**. Healthtech should enable exponential growth. Investors look for founders to have a **technology-first mindset** — not just digitizing analog workflows but building leverage into the product.

### 5. Regulatory compliance

Investors evaluate the potential **tailwinds or risks** that a solution is exposed to based on emerging regulations in healthcare records, data privacy, and licensing.

### 6. Founder quality

Fundraising is about **forging a business partnership**. Investors closely look at the leadership of the company — domain depth, integrity, ability to attract talent, and ability to navigate ambiguity.
